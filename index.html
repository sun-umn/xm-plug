Pretrained models for inverse problems

Plug-in optimization turns powerful generative priors into practical solvers.

This page traces a line of work from diffusion-model plug-ins, through temporally consistent video restoration, to foundation flow-matching priors. The shared idea is simple at a high level: keep the pretrained model intact, expose it as a prior, and solve the inverse problem around it.

Unifying theme

Use the model as a plug-in prior, not a solver that must be rewritten.

Pretrained prior

The model contributes learned structure: natural image manifolds, video semantics, temporal coherence, or broad foundation-model statistics.

Forward model

The inverse task contributes physics or degradation constraints through the measurement operator, loss, and noise model.

Optimization wrapper

Plug-in methods optimize latent seeds, warm starts, or trajectories around the pretrained model while preserving measurement fidelity.

Master equations

The plug-in view starts from MAP and moves the prior into a pretrained generator.

i

Typical MAP formulation

\[ \widehat{x}_{\mathrm{MAP}} = \arg\min_x \ell\!\left(y, \mathcal{A}(x)\right) + \lambda \Omega(x) \]

The data-fitting term enforces measurement consistency, while the regularizer encodes prior knowledge about valid solutions.

ii

Plug-in formulation

\[ \widehat{z} = \arg\min_z \ell\!\left(y, \mathcal{A}(G_\theta(z))\right) + \lambda R(z), \qquad \widehat{x} = G_\theta(\widehat{z}) \]

The pretrained model \(G_\theta\) acts as a reusable prior map, and optimization happens in the model's seed, latent, or trajectory space.

Research timeline

From domain-specific diffusion priors to reusable foundation flow priors.

01

NeurIPS 2024

DMPlug

DMPlug views the reverse diffusion process as a function and plugs it into a measurement-aware optimization problem. This separates the generative prior from the inverse-problem objective, improving both manifold feasibility and measurement feasibility, especially for nonlinear inverse problems.

  • Diffusion model prior
  • Seed-space optimization
  • Linear and nonlinear inverse problems
02

AAAI 2026 camera-ready

Temporal-Consistent Video Restoration

The video restoration work carries the plug-in perspective into video, where frame quality alone is not enough. It parameterizes video frames through diffusion seeds and adds bilevel temporal consistency: semantic consistency in seed space and pixel-level consistency through progressive optical-flow warping.

  • Video restoration
  • Temporal consistency
  • Pretrained diffusion models
03

2025 manuscript · foundation flow matching

FMPlug

FMPlug extends plug-in inverse-problem solving to foundation flow-matching models. It combines instance-guided, time-dependent warm starts with Gaussianity regularization so broad foundation priors can be steered toward task-specific reconstructions without losing the model's flow structure.

  • Foundation model prior
  • Flow matching
  • Scientific and image restoration inverse problems

Method comparisons

Two axes explain why the plug-in direction matters.

Solver strategy

Plug-in vs. interleaving

Interleaving method

Alternates reverse generative steps with measurement-correction steps. This is convenient, but correction steps can pull samples away from the learned manifold while reverse steps can weaken measurement fidelity.

Plug-in method

Treats the pretrained generative process as a differentiable prior function and optimizes around it. The inverse-problem objective can explicitly balance visual realism, data consistency, and robustness to unknown noise.

Manifold feasibility

Interleaving corrections may push samples away from the pretrained model's natural image or signal manifold.

Plug-in response

Optimize through the pretrained model map so reconstructions remain tied to the learned prior.

Measurement feasibility

Reverse generative steps can dilute hard-won progress toward satisfying the forward measurement model.

Plug-in response

Put the measurement loss directly in the outer objective and optimize it throughout reconstruction.

Noise instability

Unknown noise type or level can make handcrafted correction schedules brittle.

Plug-in response

Treat robustness as part of the optimization problem, rather than a fixed sampling-time correction rule.

Prior source

Domain-specific model vs. foundation model

Domain-specific model

Often delivers strong performance when the test distribution matches training data, but may require collecting paired or curated domain data and retraining across tasks.

Foundation model

Offers a reusable prior across broader domains. The challenge is steering it toward measurements and scientific structure without overcorrecting or breaking the foundation model's sampling geometry.

FMPlug for science

FMPlug gives foundation flow priors unprecedented reach into scientific inverse problems.

Scientific inverse problems often have scarce paired data, specialized forward operators, and measurement processes that differ sharply from everyday image restoration. FMPlug is designed for this regime: it brings broad foundation flow-matching priors into task-specific reconstruction without requiring a new domain model for every scientific problem.
